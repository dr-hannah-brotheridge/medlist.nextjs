Search

Enter your search term into the search bar above and select from the drop-down list. To go directly to monographs or interactions, click on the “monograph” or “stockleys interactions” links in the drop-down list.

If the search term is found in a monograph, the results will indicate which section of the monograph the term is in so you can browse the results quicker.

Search Indications

To search for a specific indication, enter your search term into the search bar above and select from the drop-down list.

The drop-down list is a condensed list from “Search NZF”, where the search term is found only in the indications field. This is designed to give you more direct search results.

Interactions checker

Enter a medicine into the interactions search bar above and select from the drop-down list. Add medicines one at a time to build your search. Remove medicines with the backspace key. Hold down the backspace key to quickly remove medicines from your search. For further information about the interaction category, hover over the coloured box, and the dashed underline text in the “action”, "severity" and "evidence" columns.

Individual medicine checking cannot currently be done for a trade product (e.g.) Panadol. These need to be performed as a generic product (e.g. paracetamol) or by adding specific products to perform a drug-drug interaction.

Interaction information is provided by Stockley's Interaction Alerts. Pharmacodynamic interactions compounded by two or more drugs with similar actions may not be included in the search results; knowledge of the adverse reaction profile of all drugs is important when selecting treatment options—see individual drug monographs. For more information on interactions see Drug Interactions.

Compatibility of intravenous and other parenteral formulations

This Interactions checker does not address the compatibility, stability, storage, and preparation of intravenous or other parenteral drugs. Please refer to local resources for information when assessing compatibility with diluting agents and infusion fluids, or administering multiple parenteral preparations.
